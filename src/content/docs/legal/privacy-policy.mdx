# NanoKnow Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** May 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how NanoKnow, Inc. (“NanoKnow,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, and shares information when you use the NanoKnow website, application, and related services.

## 1. Information We Collect

### Information You Provide

We collect information you provide directly, including:

- Name  
- Email address  
- Affiliation (e.g., university, company, lab)  
- Profile or account information  
- Content you submit (documents, papers, datasets, notes, comments, metadata)

You may browse parts of NanoKnow without registering.

### Account Verification

If you register, we may verify your email using a unique confirmation link. This confirms that you control the email address.

### Usage and Technical Data

We automatically collect certain information when you use NanoKnow:

- IP address  
- Browser type and device information  
- Pages viewed and interactions  
- Referring URLs  
- Timestamps and session activity  

We may store server logs containing IP addresses for security, debugging, and operational purposes.

### Content and Metadata

If you upload documents or data, we may extract and generate:

- Metadata (e.g., tags, categories, tools, processes)  
- Structural information (sections, headings)  
- Derived representations (e.g., embeddings, indexes)

## 2. How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

- Operate and maintain the platform  
- Enable account creation and authentication  
- Provide, organize, and display content  
- Improve search, discovery, and knowledge organization  
- Process uploaded materials using automated systems (including AI)  
- Personalize user experience  
- Respond to support requests  
- Communicate updates, notifications, and relevant platform information  
- Improve platform performance, usability, and security  

## 3. AI and Automated Processing

NanoKnow may use automated systems, including artificial intelligence and machine learning, to:

- Analyze uploaded content  
- Extract metadata and structure  
- Generate summaries or descriptions  
- Enable semantic search and recommendations  
- Organize knowledge across the platform  

These systems may produce imperfect or incomplete results and should not be relied upon without independent verification.

## 4. How We Share Information

We do **not sell personal information**.

We may share information in the following situations:

### With Other Users / Public

Content you submit may be visible to other users or, in some cases, publicly accessible, depending on platform design and settings.

### With Service Providers

We use trusted third-party providers (e.g., hosting, storage, authentication, analytics, AI processing). These providers process data on our behalf.

### For Legal Reasons

We may disclose information if required to:

- comply with applicable law  
- respond to legal requests  
- protect rights, safety, or property  
- enforce our Terms  

### Aggregated Data

We may share anonymized or aggregated data that cannot reasonably identify you.

## 5. Cookies and Tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

- maintain sessions and authentication  
- remember user preferences  
- understand site usage  
- improve platform performance  

You can control cookies through your browser settings.

## 6. Retention of Your Information

We keep your information for the purposes for which it is processed. The length of time for which we retain information depends on the purpose for which we collected and use it and/or as required to comply with applicable laws.

## 7. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational safeguards to protect your information, including:

- access controls  
- encryption where appropriate  
- secure infrastructure providers  

However, no system is completely secure. Your use of NanoKnow indicates your agreement to assume the risk. NanoKnow is not responsible for any acts or omissions of any unauthorized third parties.

## 8. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights under laws such as the GDPR and CCPA/CPRA.

These may include the right to:

- access your personal information  
- correct inaccurate information  
- request deletion  
- restrict or object to processing  
- request data portability  
- opt out of certain data uses  

To exercise these rights, contact:  
**admin@nanoknow.org**

We will respond as required by applicable law.

## 9. International Users

NanoKnow may process and store data in the United States or other jurisdictions. By using the platform, you understand that your data may be transferred to locations with different data protection laws.

## 10. Children’s Privacy

NanoKnow is not intended for children under 13. If you are under 13, do not use the platform.

If we become aware that personal data has been collected from a child without appropriate consent, we will take steps to delete it.

## 11. Third-Party Links

NanoKnow may link to external sites. We are not responsible for their privacy practices.

## 12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will post updates on this page and revise the “Last updated” date.

## 13. Disputes; No Rights of Third Parties

If you choose to access NanoKnow, any dispute over privacy is subject to our [Terms of Service](/legal/terms-of-service/), including limitations on damages, resolution of disputes by binding arbitration, and application of the laws of the United States and the State of New York. This privacy policy does not create rights enforceable by third parties.
