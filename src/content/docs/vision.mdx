  <p>
    Across the United States, academic and government nanofabrication facilities support research, training, prototyping, and technology development. Each facility develops valuable local expertise, but that knowledge is often difficult to find beyond the people, documents, and systems where it was first created.
  </p>
  <p>
    NanoKnow envisions a connected national ecosystem where fabrication knowledge can move more easily across institutions — helping labs learn from one another, reduce duplicated effort, train the next generation, and accelerate the path from research discovery to real-world technology.
  </p>
  <p>

  </p>

<Section title='<span style="color: var(--color-lightmode-primary);">A Shared Challenge</span>'>

<div class="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-4xl mx-auto mb-16 mt-10 text-lg text-left">
  <p>
    The map shows a broad national ecosystem of facilities with different tools, missions, users, and institutional contexts. Yet many of these labs face similar challenges: documenting processes, training users, troubleshooting tools, preserving staff expertise, and helping researchers find reliable fabrication knowledge.
  </p>
  <p>
    When this knowledge remains isolated, facilities spend time and resources solving similar problems independently. NanoKnow's vision is to create a common knowledge layer that helps local expertise become more discoverable, reusable, and useful across the broader community.
  </p>
  <p>
    This shared layer preserves context, attribution, and source information while making practical knowledge easier to find and build upon.
  </p>
</div>

</Section>

<Section title='<span style="color: var(--color-lightmode-primary);">The Opportunity Ahead</span>'>

<div class="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-4xl mx-auto mb-16 mt-10 text-lg text-left">
  <p>
    The future of U.S. semiconductor, quantum, and nanotechnology innovation depends not only on advanced tools, but on the ability to share expertise, train skilled people, and move knowledge across institutions. NanoKnow helps build that connective layer — turning distributed facility knowledge into a resource that can accelerate research, strengthen the workforce, and support technology translation.
  </p>
</div>
