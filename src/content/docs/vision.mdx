  <p>
    Across the United States, academic and government nanofabrication facilities generate enormous practical knowledge every day — from tool operation and process development to training, troubleshooting, safety, and research support. Yet much of this knowledge remains local, scattered across individual labs, documents, websites, and staff experience.
  </p>
  <p>
    NanoKnow envisions an open, AI-enabled knowledge platform that connects this distributed expertise so that labs of all sizes can share, discover, and reuse fabrication knowledge across institutional boundaries.
  </p>

  <p>
    This map shows <a href="/facilities/" style="color: #1354A2; text-decoration: underline;">172 academic and government nanofabrication facilities across the United States</a>. Together, they illustrate the breadth of the national nanofabrication ecosystem and the need for shared infrastructure that can connect knowledge, documentation, data, and expertise across institutions.
  </p>
  <p>
    Across this ecosystem, much of the knowledge needed to operate tools, train users, develop processes, troubleshoot failures, and support researchers remains distributed across individual facilities. While each lab has unique capabilities and local expertise, many face similar challenges and often recreate solutions independently.
  </p>
  <p>
    NanoKnow's vision is to provide an open, shared knowledge layer for this community. By connecting documentation, process knowledge, training resources, data, and expert insight through an AI-enabled platform, NanoKnow can help facilities preserve local expertise while making reusable knowledge easier to find, share, and build upon.
  </p>
  <p>
    The goal is a platform open to labs of all sizes and missions — academic, government, nonprofit, and community-serving — so that nanofabrication knowledge can move more easily across institutional boundaries.
  </p>
</div>
